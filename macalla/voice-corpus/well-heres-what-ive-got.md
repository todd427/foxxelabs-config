Well, here's what I've got:

“Forward, Not Back” -- that’s my motto for what comes next.

My story is about starting up the internet, getting an Amazon.com warehouse on-line with electric delivery drones and starting to rebuild.  Longer term, the container Port of Los Angeles needs to be secured and container ships re-started and automated.  Robots everywhere.

1. I'm going to start the internet back up.  There will be many ways to start it.  I’m sure that several smart people (like those banker dudes) will have convinced others to make a “just in case” backup.  It’s not as big as you think.

a.	Getting the internet back up reconnects us to a huge source of cultural capital (think of all those on-line courses, for starters).

i.	This “resurrection” will be phased. One central site can emulate the pre-ZA apocalypse (you don’t have that many users).

ii.	I’m thinking Kwajalein -- if they’re keeping the satellites on orbit, they’re going to be some smart cookies.

2. I'm going to have someone open up one (and then more) of the Amazon.com warehouses (the one in the best condition).

3. We're going to activate the drones.  They'll be the delivery system.

	I. We’re going to need a lot of diapers RSN

4. Near term (1-10 years):

1. The container Port of Los Angeles and as many container ships as possible are the best solution to so many problems.  Getting the logistics systems up so that containers can be cataloged and selected will be a chore.

2. Reconstruction of space launch capabilities.  I’m thinking that this, too, won’t be as hard as some people may think.  Probably air-launch systems to start but perhaps some SpaceX rockets.

1.	It’s a morale booster.

2.	It’s needed to maintain global contact.

5. Longer term, switch to solar/wind.  Use battery and pumped storage.  Also, you need to “go forward” not back.  Forget roads, bridges, dams, big cities.  Build new communities (I’m thinking Maine) which are highly automated -- you’ve a labor shortage, you’re going to have to automate.

The US had a $21 trillion GDP in 2019.  Given that and a total post-ZA population of ~3 million, the per capita “wealth” (you gotta get it) is roughly $7 million.

The biggest problem isn’t wealth, it’s survival and sustainability.

You have a population pinch in the 0-18 age ranges as well as the 50+ ranges.  You already have a mini-population bomb but your potential mothers are aging out (assume menopause at 40).  That means at max gestation rate (women as breeders, to which I’m opposed) which is roughly 18 months (9 months gestation, 9 months lactation) each 20 year old woman could, IF LUCKY (for some barbaric definitions of “lucky”) have 13 children in their 20 years of reproduction (I doubt those “mothers” would actually live that long).

	I can see families of 5-6 kids being outside the norm, families of 4 being more likely.

	With roughly 1.5 million age-range bearing mothers, that comes to 6 million new children in the next 20 years or so (at which point some of those will become mothers).

	So this would mean that the population triples in 20 years (more or less, you’re now seeing the older folk tottering).
