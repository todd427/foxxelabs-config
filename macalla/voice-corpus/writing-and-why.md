Writing… and why

So, one of the things that I learned over time about my mother was that when she was really proud of something she was working on, she’d come up with a reason (excuse) to get me to read it early. I don’t know if she ever figured that out herself but… there it is.

So she had technical questions for me on Freedom’s Landing (highly recommended, by the way) and this continued until she finished the fourth book Freedom’s Ransom (where  I enlisted my dentist to give her dental tech).

	Now when she was done with that book, I said, “You know… you’ve got a really great opening for a whole bunch of books here. Why don’t you write those?”

	And she said words to the effect of, “No, I’ve written it out. I don’t want to write a new series. I want to try something else.”

	Now, some fifty books later, I get her point.  My mother wrote the things that moved her, told the stories that were important to her.  She boldly went where no person had gone before.

	I do much the same.  When we’d written Sky Dragons, the eighth book in the Third Pass on Pern, Mum said that we needed a break. I finished City of Angels (which, because there are a zillion books about L.A., I changed to Ellay: The First AI) and went on to finish The Jupiter Game.

	Writing is all about exploring strange new worlds or exploring interesting ideas. It’s about having fun while writing.  I feel that unless you like what you’re writing, you won’t get a good book -- it might hit all the plot points but the drive won’t be there.

	More than that, I feel that all books are about transformation -- about change.  As the first person to boldly go into a new world, the writer is the person who changes first. As Captain Jack Sparrow would say, “Now, bring me that new horizon.”

	So I’ve finished the Canaris Rift series… for now. 

I’ve finished The Steam World series… for now. 

I’ve finished the L.A. Witch series… for now.

	What am I doing now?  I’m trying something new. I’m writing in the world of paranormal romance (PNR). Steamy paranormal romance. And because it’s such a huge departure, because Amazon tends to recommend books by the same authors, I have unleashed a pen name (a pseudonym). 

I don’t want those who love my Middle Grade L.A. Witch series to get grossed out by my steamy romance books.
