We stand at a crossroads: a nation divided amongst itself teeters on the edge of a civil war.  A world that clings to old ways of thinking and old politics trying to deal with the reality of simple science: that adding heat to any process will accelerate it.

We need something to unite the people of the United States in a way that comes rarely to us.  Something that gives us a sense of wonder, a sense of hope, and a common goal.

We need to hear those voices that have fallen back to racism, white supremacy, fundamentalism and speak to their core fears -- that they are being left behind, that a country where whites are not in the majority will become a country which despises whites.

At the same time we do this great nation a huge disservice by disenfranchising that vibrant voice that is the black culture residing in our nation.  We need to teach people to fear fear and to embrace the new, the alien.

If we are to survive, as a nation, even as a species, we must move forward.  To embrace the past would be to accept the death of billions of people -- our world cannot feed those living on it and go back to the ways of feudalism.

I say that the way forward is this: we choose to become a space-faring nation.  That we embark upon a great journey to not only explore space but to profit from it.

The Artemis project, a return to the Moon, is only scratching the surface.

What if we could move some of our most polluting industries off the planet? What if we could mine the asteroids? What if we could take up permanent residence in space?

We know, after over twenty years with the International Space Station, that living in microgravity is not a viable solution.  But we have also known for over fifty years that we have the technology to produce large rotating space stations which would generate artificial gravity -- that we can build habitats where humans can live in the same gravity they experience here on Earth.

I think that we should accept this challenge -- to build a rotating space station, a large space station capable of housing hundreds of people with the view to building even larger space habitats capable of housing thousands and tens of thousands of people.

We could move industry to nearby habitats in space without regard to their pollution, we could create new industries using the natural resources of space -- including even the vacuum of space -- and we could expand our knowledge of our planet, our cosmos, with these efforts.

We would also be able to challenge ourselves to completely understand sustainable systems -- because a habitable space station is a world in microcosm.  Our explorations would lead us to solutions applicable here on Earth.

At the same time, we would expand our technological abilities and our frontiers.  It is one thing to put a man on the moon for a week, quite another to put a town in space permanently. And even more to plan on settling the planets of our solar system, of creating a new frontier for America.
