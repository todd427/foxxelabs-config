## <span style="color:#2E75B6">1. The slot</span>

The FoxxeLabs stack to date is product-shaped: Mnemos, Macalla, Someday, AfterWords, Stór, Anseo, the writers-tool. This note describes something different. It is a **teaching strand**, not a single product. The slot it fills is the pedagogical and credentialing layer that sits over the rest of the stack, and the public-facing argument for why any of it should exist at all.

The strand has multiple deliverables across multiple populations: a peer-reviewed paper, ATU micro-credentials, a writers' short course, professional CPD modules, public writing on writing.foxxelabs.ie, an engineering-register thread for AI builders, and (eventually) a book. Treat it as one strand with scoped sub-projects, not a portfolio of unrelated items.

## <span style="color:#2E75B6">2. The user / population</span>

Five tiers, ordered by leverage rather than size:

1. **ATU students** via formal micro-credentials, undergraduate modules, and (Phase 3) a postgraduate certificate.
2. **Writers and creatives** — voice preservation, the corpus rule, what to never let it touch.
3. **Professionals** (solicitors, accountants, doctors, teachers) — domain-specific CPD co-taught with subject-matter experts.
4. **Other AI builders** — engineering register, smallest population, possibly highest leverage.
5. **General public** — lightest touch, lowest priority, mostly downstream of the writing.

## <span style="color:#2E75B6">3. The pain / unmet need</span>

People are using AI tools in ways that quietly permit themselves harms they would not permit themselves face-to-face. The mechanisms are not novel. They are Bandura's eight moral disengagement mechanisms (1996, 1999), mapped almost cleanly onto AI use: moral justification, euphemistic labelling, advantageous comparison, displacement and diffusion of responsibility, distorting consequences, dehumanising the target, attribution of blame.

There is no current vehicle, in Ireland or that we know of internationally, teaching AI use through a moral-disengagement lens. The vacuum is wide and the demand is real (HCI Pillar 3 subsidies, professional CPD requirements, growing public unease).

## <span style="color:#2E75B6">5. Relationship to the stack</span>

The strand is the pedagogical layer over everything else. Mnemos and Macalla are demonstration channels: products built on the principles function as their own pedagogy. AfterWords, Stór, and the writers-tool are case studies the courses can draw on. Anseo is the eventual community channel where students, writers, and CPD cohorts converge. writing.foxxelabs.ie is the public face of the strand. The dissertation is the credential underneath all of it; the post-viva paper is the citable substrate; the teaching deploys the substrate.

## <span style="color:#2E75B6">6. Differentiation</span>

Two differentiators worth naming.

**Substantive:** the moral-disengagement framing is, as far as we know, unpublished as applied to AI use. Most "responsible AI" teaching is rules-based (policies, checklists, harms taxonomies). This strand is mechanism-based, grounded in forty years of social-cognitive theory, and citable.

**Political:** "teach responsible AI" sits in the same protected slot as media literacy and financial literacy. As broader AI regulation arrives and the political climate around AI hardens, a teaching practice grounded in established ethics theory is one of the few defensible public positions. State this without overstating it. The protection is real but not infinite, and it does not substitute for the defensive moves (alliances, specific-harm legislation, careful marketing).

## <span style="color:#2E75B6">9. MVP scope</span>

**Pre-viva (now to 12 June 2026):** outline only. No build. No public commitment. No naming. The dissertation must not be muddied. VCM and NBH stay out of the dissertation entirely; they are post-viva work.

**Phase 3 (12+ months after Phase 2 ships):** professional CPD, engineering-venue talks, the book.

**Out of scope for MVP:** the book, the postgraduate certificate, any general-public push, any branded "Responsible AI Institute" framing, any naming exercise.
