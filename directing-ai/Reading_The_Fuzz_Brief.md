# <span style="color:#1B4F72">Reading the Fuzz</span>
### <span style="color:#16A085">The recognition discipline at the heart of AI orchestration</span>

<span style="color:#7F8C8D">**Brief — May 2026** · Author: Todd McCaffrey · NFQ Level 9 · 10 ECTS · 10-week studio</span>
<span style="color:#7F8C8D">**Course 1 of the *Orchestrated Cognition* programme** · Standalone-credentialed; required prerequisite for Course 2</span>

---

## <span style="color:#1B4F72">What the course teaches</span>

*Reading the Fuzz* teaches the recognition skill that every other orchestration skill depends on: identifying what kind of uncertainty a professional situation primarily presents, articulating it precisely enough to work with, and refusing to defer to a tool's confident-sounding output when judgement is the orchestrator's job.

This is the layer most current AI training skips. Courses teach learners *how to use the tools*; this course teaches learners *how to read the situation* the tools are being used in. Without the reading, the tools mislead. With it, they become directable.

## <span style="color:#1B4F72">The central claim</span>

Fuzzy professional situations are not all the same kind of fuzzy. Each kind rewards different responses and punishes the others'. A clinician running a policy analyst's playbook misses the patient's deterioration. A documentary director running a clinician's playbook makes the obvious film. The recognition skill — *which kind of fuzz am I in?* — is the load-bearing professional capacity AI tooling does not provide and frequently undermines.

## <span style="color:#1B4F72">The six anchor cases</span>

Recognition is taught through six composite figures drawn from real professional work:

- <span style="color:#34495E">**Niamh**</span>, a policy analyst with four days to produce a position paper on a topic she has to define as she works — *under-specified fuzz with discoverable structure*
- <span style="color:#34495E">**Cormac**</span>, a consultant cardiologist taking a phone call at 11pm about a deteriorating patient — *time-pressured fuzz with irreversible action*
- <span style="color:#34495E">**Saoirse**</span>, a documentary director with 40 hours of footage and no film yet — *generative fuzz with no out-there answer*
- <span style="color:#34495E">**Aoife**</span>, a senior counsel preparing cross-examination from 4,000 pages against a witness who has been prepped — *adversarial fuzz with a moving counterparty*
- <span style="color:#34495E">**Ronan**</span>, a startup founder asking "what are we actually building?" with co-founders, team, and investors all holding different answers — *collective fuzz with self-implicating choice*
- <span style="color:#34495E">**Méabh**</span>, the 47-year-old daughter of an 81-year-old father after a possible TIA — *personal-stakes fuzz where tools shape the question*

Learners come to recognise these as patterns the way medical students come to recognise classic clinical presentations. By the end of the course, they can read their own professional situations against the six, identify the dominant fuzz and any secondaries, and act on the reading.

## <span style="color:#1B4F72">The spine — discipline against Borrowed Authority</span>

Across all six cases, a single failure mode recurs: *Borrowed Authority* — letting a tool's confident output substitute for a judgement only the orchestrator can make. Two weeks of the course are devoted explicitly to building the catch-myself reflex against it. The discipline graduates carry forward is the most durable thing the course teaches.

## <span style="color:#1B4F72">Course structure</span>

Ten weeks, studio model, master critique throughout:

- <span style="color:#34495E">**Weeks 1–2:**</span> The Six — anchor cases introduced; learners place their own current work against the typology
- <span style="color:#34495E">**Weeks 3–4:**</span> Hybrids and Mis-Framings — reading mixed situations; recognising when the question form itself is wrong
- <span style="color:#34495E">**Weeks 5–6:**</span> Articulating the Fuzz — naming what is unclear so it can be worked on, without prematurely collapsing it
- <span style="color:#34495E">**Weeks 7–8:**</span> Borrowed Authority — sustained practice in catching the pull of confident-sounding output
- <span style="color:#34495E">**Week 9:**</span> Small Build — a single contained orchestration exercise demonstrating that recognition has become operational
- <span style="color:#34495E">**Week 10:**</span> Recognition Defence — public defence of a real fuzz reading from the learner's current work

## <span style="color:#1B4F72">Assessment</span>

No examinations. Fuzz Portfolio (30%) · Hybrid Critique Exercises (20%) · Articulated-Fuzz Documents (15%) · Borrowed Authority Detection Log (10%) · Small Build (15%) · Recognition Defence (10%).

## <span style="color:#1B4F72">What graduates take away</span>

A felt recognition vocabulary applied to their own domain. The discipline to resist *Borrowed Authority* in professional practice. A documented reading of a real situation from their own current work, defended in front of cohort and external critics. A Level 9 micro-credential at 10 ECTS, stackable toward *Orchestrating Under Fuzz* (Course 2) and onward toward a Postgraduate Certificate.

## <span style="color:#1B4F72">Audience</span>

Mid-career professionals who exercise independent judgement in their domain — analysts, researchers, clinicians, lawyers, designers, managers, journalists, senior individual contributors in regulated fields. People who use AI tools daily and have started to feel where the tools mislead them but cannot yet articulate why. No coding background required.

This course is also appropriate as a standalone credential for senior practitioners who have substantial execution chops and need the reading layer without committing to the full studio sequence in Course 2.

## <span style="color:#1B4F72">Prerequisites</span>

Level 8 primary degree or equivalent professional standing. Minimum three years' post-qualification professional experience. Working operational fluency with at least one AI tool, demonstrated through a one-page admissions exercise (place a recent piece of your own work against one of the six fuzz types and articulate the fuzz). Capacity for 20–25 hours per week over 10 weeks. Admission by portfolio route available.

## <span style="color:#1B4F72">Format</span>

NFQ Level 9 · 10 ECTS · 10-week studio · Cohort up to 20 · Studio sessions plus independent build, critique, and reflection work · Master critique throughout · Final defence in front of cohort and invited external critics.
