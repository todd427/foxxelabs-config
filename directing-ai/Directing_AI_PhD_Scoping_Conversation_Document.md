## <span style="color:#1B4F72">The work I'm already doing</span>

Over the past eighteen months I've been authoring a coherent body of design work around what I'm calling *Directing AI* — a discipline of directing AI systems, human specialists, and professional judgement toward outcomes the orchestrator never executes themselves. The work draws on Christopher Alexander's pattern-language tradition, Donald Schön's reflection-in-action, and the studio-pedagogy tradition (architectural training, surgical residency, the writers' workshop).

The current portfolio comprises:

- A typology of six recognisable kinds of professional uncertainty (the *fuzz typology*)
- A pattern language of approximately twenty-five starter patterns and twelve anti-patterns, with one particularly load-bearing anti-pattern (*Borrowed Authority* — the failure mode of letting a tool's confident output substitute for a judgement only the human can make)
- A case library of six anchor cases drawn from professional practice (clinical, legal, policy, creative, business, personal-stakes)
- A pedagogical model and curriculum design for credentialed programmes (undergraduate minor, Postgraduate Certificate, MSc, with PhD pathway implied)
- A strategic case for *Directing AI* as a forming academic discipline

The work is at the stage where the design documents are complete and the next step is producing the foundational artefacts that would make the discipline academically real — pattern catalogue published in canonical form, case library at depth, textbook in circulation, empirical evidence of the methodology applied in real research.

That foundational work is the substance of what I'm proposing as the PhD.

## <span style="color:#1B4F72">Miteo as empirical vehicle</span>

In parallel with the *Directing AI* design work, I've been scoping a mitochondrial-research programme I'm calling Miteo — a genuine biological-research programme on mitochondrial function, conducted as an interdisciplinary outsider using AI tools and deliberate methodology to compensate for my lack of native biological expertise. Miteo has been parked through the MSc but resumes post-viva.

Miteo is itself a *Directing AI* example. The mitochondrial research is real research (substantive questions about mitochondrial function, real literature engagement, real publishable outputs as the goal), and the methodology by which the research proceeds is deliberate application of the orchestration framework I'm developing. The two are not separate projects competing for attention; they are the same work seen from two angles.

This is relevant to the PhD scoping because Miteo provides an empirical case in which the methodology is applied to a substantive question by the methodology's founding author, with outputs and process both documented. That kind of practice-led case is unusual and valuable as PhD material.

## <span style="color:#1B4F72">Four candidate topic framings</span>

The PhD could be scoped in at least four different ways, each with different supervisory implications. I have views on which framing is strongest but I want to discuss this with you rather than arrive at it alone.

<span style="color:#34495E">**Framing A — Methodology PhD with Miteo as empirical vehicle.**</span>

Working title: *Directing AI as a Methodological Discipline: Theoretical Framework and Empirical Application in Interdisciplinary Research.*

Thesis claim: *Directing AI* is a coherent methodological discipline with characteristic patterns, recognisable failure modes, and observable outcomes; the methodology produces defensible scholarly work when applied to research in domains outside the researcher's native expertise. Miteo is the empirical demonstration.

Strengths: aligns with the work I'm already doing; produces both methodology and substantive science as outputs; the empirical case is already underway.

Risks: broad scope requires careful boundary-setting; mitochondrial biology element requires supplementary supervisory coverage.

<span style="color:#34495E">**Framing B — Cyberpsychology PhD on Borrowed Authority.**</span>

Working title: *Borrowed Authority and the Reflective Director: A Cyberpsychological Study of Human Cognitive Sovereignty in AI-Assisted Professional Work.*

Thesis claim: AI tooling introduces a characteristic cognitive failure mode (*Borrowed Authority*) into professional practice; the failure mode is empirically observable, theoretically grounded in existing cyberpsychological literature, and resistible through identifiable counter-practices.

Strengths: sits cleanly within cyberpsychology; the *Borrowed Authority* construct has direct connection to existing literature on cognitive automation, heteronomy, and trust calibration; empirical work is methodologically standard for the field.

Risks: narrower than the discipline-building ambition; treats the whole orchestration framework as background rather than foreground.

<span style="color:#34495E">**Framing C — Pattern Acquisition Study.**</span>

Working title: *Acquisition of an Orchestration Pattern Language in Professional Practice: A Mixed-Methods Study.*

Thesis claim: orchestration patterns can be acquired through structured practice; the acquisition process is observable and varies in identifiable ways between practitioners; effective pattern acquisition correlates with reduced *Borrowed Authority* manifestation.

Strengths: methodologically rigorous; produces findings of direct pedagogical relevance; cleanly fundable through standard educational-research mechanisms.

Risks: requires running courses that produce data, which is operationally harder given the timing of credentialed-programme development; depends on cohort access I don't yet have.

<span style="color:#34495E">**Framing D — Practice-Led PhD with Reflection as Method.**</span>

Working title: *Founding a Discipline: A Practice-Led Study of Directing AI Through Methodological Documentation.*

Thesis claim: the practice of authoring a discipline is itself a research method; documented practice produces transferable knowledge that contributes to both the discipline being authored and the broader literature on practice-led inquiry.

Strengths: most directly captures the actual work I'm doing; Schön's reflective-practice tradition supports this methodologically; produces the foundational artefacts (catalogue, case library, textbook) as the PhD output.

Risks: practice-led PhDs are established in arts, design, and education contexts but less so in cyberpsychology; would require institutional advocacy and a methodologically generous supervisory team.

My current leaning is toward **Framing A or D**, with elements of B woven in. A has the cleanest topic-statement; D best captures what I'm actually doing. B is a natural fallback if the more ambitious framings prove institutionally difficult.

## <span style="color:#1B4F72">Three candidate supervision structures</span>

The work spans cyberpsychology, applied AI / computing, research methodology, and philosophy of practice — with biology added if Miteo is in scope. No single supervisor covers all of this, so the question is structural: how is supervision distributed?

<span style="color:#34495E">**Structure 1 — You as primary supervisor.**</span>

Natural extension of the MSc relationship. You provide the supervisory home and the cyberpsychology framing; one or two co-supervisors cover the methodological breadth and (if relevant) the biology. Most institutionally straightforward.

Question to discuss: do you have bandwidth and appetite for primary supervision on this scope?

<span style="color:#34495E">**Structure 2 — You as institutional sponsor, primary supervision elsewhere.**</span>

The PhD sits institutionally in a different ATU area (computing, applied AI, or interdisciplinary research methodology) with you as a co-supervisor providing the cyberpsychology framing and institutional continuity with my MSc work. Protects your bandwidth while keeping you in the room.

Question to discuss: which ATU colleagues would be appropriate primary supervisors, and what's the realistic process for placing the PhD with them?

<span style="color:#34495E">**Structure 3 — Multi-supervisor with equal standing.**</span>

Three equal supervisors covering cyberpsychology, methodology, and (computing or biology depending on framing). Heavier institutional setup; pedagogically right for genuinely interdisciplinary topics.

Question to discuss: does ATU's doctoral structure support equal-standing multi-supervisor arrangements, and which colleagues would form a viable supervisory team?
